Governments Around The World Are Taking Notice

From Asia to Europe to North America

When Star Wars: Battlefront 2 released, people criticized its explicit use of gambling and unfair advantage gained by gambling a lot, all within a game whose target audience is teenagers. Governments across the world started to take notice as citizens wrote to their representatives requesting action be taken. China has policy that requires game companies to publicize probabilities of loot box items; they also must have some actual value in-game and not merely a probability of value. The Netherlands have discussed the idea of investigating gambling practices in video games. The following video is a highlight reel from a public announcement in Hawaii requesting action and legislation against gambling in video games. If you are concerned about gambling in video games, please write to your Member of Provincial Parliament (MPP) and Member of Parliament (MP) requesting action from Canada.

Definitions

Loot Box (pictured above, left): an in-game item that unlocks other random in-game items and has a small chance of rare and valueable items appearing.

Microtransactions (pictured above, right): in-game purchases that unlock specific in-game items or currencies.
