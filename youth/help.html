Real Gambling in Video Games

Gambling Can Be Addicting

Recognizing A Problem

If you think you or a close friend or family member is suffering from an addiction to video games or is investing too much money into microtransactions, look for these symptoms:

● Playing in secret or lying about how long he or she has been playing
● Mulling over video games when engrossed in other activities
● Sacrificing time at work or school in order to play
● Using games to escape from reality
● Anxiety or depression
● Becoming alienated from friends and family
● Becoming irritated if unable to play
● Lacking enough hours of sleep
● Losing interest in other activities and hobbies
● Increasingly ignoring personal hygiene
Source

Video game addiction is often a result of other pressures in life, rather than addictive elements in games. Gambling is habit forming and should be used in moderation. For help with both, talk to someone you trust about it such as a guidance counselor or parent. You can call the Ontario Problem Gambling Helpline at 1-888-230-3505 or visit their website for more information.
