Loot Boxes and Microtransactions

Many apps and games use loot boxes and microtransactions to enhance or speed-up gameplay. In some games, a playerâ€™s progression may feel so tedious that microtransactions become a necessary component to experiencing the game. For example, Overwatch is a very popular game that costs $40-60 to buy and then has the option to buy bundles of loot boxes in-game. The most expensive bundle is 50 loot boxes for $50. The player can also receive 50 loot boxes by leveling their character 50 levels. It takes roughly one hour to earn a loot box so 50 hours or $50 - your choice. Furthermore, the loot box system does not guarantee that you will get what you want; this is why it is gambling. You purchase a chance at winning a desired outcome. Definitions

Loot Box (pictured above, left): an in-game item that unlocks other random in-game items and has a small chance of rare and valueable items appearing.

Microtransactions (pictured above, right): in-game purchases that unlock specific in-game items or currencies.
