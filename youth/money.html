Gambling in Video Games Resource Centre

Your Money Or Your Time

A 21st Century Problem

The concept of loot boxes and microtransactions are nearly identical to slot machines and casino tokens, respectively. Together, they are gambling: a user spends real money on a small chance they will gain an item of greater value. Young people, ages 4-17, have free access to apps and games that use gambling. Jack Black recently spoke about his own six year old son spending $3000 on microtransactions before being caught. Some very young kids do not know that these in-game items cost real money. Teenagers and young adults can feel compelled to gamble in video games to gain valuable in-game items just because they really enjoy the game. Anyone who chooses to gamble should expect to lose more than they win; gambling is not worth the investment. If you want a specific item, do not gamble for it, you are likely to not receive it.

Definitions

Loot Box (pictured above, left): an in-game item that unlocks other random in-game items and has a small chance of rare and valueable items appearing.

Microtransactions (pictured above, right): in-game purchases that unlock specific in-game items or currencies.
