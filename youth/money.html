Real Gambling in Video Games

Your Money Or Your Time

A 21st Century Problem

The concept of loot boxes and microtransactions are nearly identical to slot machines and casino tokens, respectively. They are a form of gambling: a user spends real money on a chance they will gain an item of greater value where the probability of a win is less than 50%. Young people, ages 4-17, have free access to apps and games that use gambling. Jack Black recently spoke about his own six year old son spending $3000 on microtransactions before being caught. Some very young kids do not know that these in-game items cost real money. For teenagers and young adults, gambling in video games has a chance to form a gambling addiction or habit that could continue into adulthood. Anyone who chooses to gamble should expect to lose more than they win; gambling is not worth the investment.
